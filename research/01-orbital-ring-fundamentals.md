# Orbital Ring Fundamentals

## Sources
- Grokipedia/Wikipedia mirror on Orbital Rings
- Paul Birch (1982-1983) JBIS papers "Orbital Ring Systems and Jacob's Ladders"
- NSS Orbital Rings paper

## Core Concept
- Artificial ring around Earth rotating faster than orbital velocity
- Net outward centrifugal force counteracts gravity + supports loads
- Stationary platforms ("skyhooks") electromagnetically coupled to spinning rotor
- Short elevator cables ("Jacob's Ladders") hang down to surface
- NOT a space elevator — much shorter tethers, existing materials work

## Key Parameters
- **Altitude**: 300-600 km (LEO)
- **Rotor velocity**: 8-10 km/s (orbital velocity ~7.8 km/s at equator)
- **Ring diameter**: ~40,000 km (Earth's circumference)
- **Ring width**: 1-10 meters
- **Total mass**: ~10^8 tons
- **Tether length**: 300-600 km

## Physics
- Centrifugal acceleration: a = v²/r
- Angular deflection at supports: Δθ = 4ΔH/(R + H)
- Magnetic levitation force: F_L = (I²/z₀) × (μ₀a/4π)
- Counter-rotating streams → zero net angular momentum
- Precession synchronized with Earth's rotation

## Materials
- **Kevlar cables**: yield stress Y/ρ = 230 km²/s²
- **Aluminum platforms**: conductivity ~3.54 × 10⁷ Ω⁻¹ m⁻¹
- **Superconducting coils**: Nb₃Sn, ~3.5 T fields
- **Future**: carbon nanotubes (theoretical 100 GPa), graphite whiskers
- **Tensile strength needed**: 3-20 GPa

## Energy & Launch
- Launch energy: ~62 MJ/kg (vs 30-60 MJ/kg chemical rockets)
- Launch capacity: ~10⁵ tons/year
- Linear induction motors → 8 km/s
- Maintenance power: ~0.2 GW (atmospheric drag offset)
- Cost: potentially $0.05/kg (vs $2,000/kg current)

## Construction (Bootstrap Approach)
1. Small-scale orbital test rings via conventional rockets
2. Minimal viable system (thin rotating wire) → expand using ring's own levitation
3. In-orbit factories with automated robotics fabricate segments
4. Iterative scaling

## Key Engineering Challenges (SIMULATION TARGETS)
1. **Orbital mechanics**: precession control, counter-rotating elements, velocity management
2. **Structural**: tensile strength requirements, stress distribution in tapered cables
3. **Magnetic levitation**: superconducting coil dynamics, coupling between rotor and platform
4. **Atmospheric drag**: momentum replenishment, 5-year decay at 300km during solar max
5. **Space debris**: Kessler syndrome risk, point defense systems
6. **Vibration/oscillation**: coupled oscillations, vibrational mode damping
7. **Alignment**: precision across thousands of km
8. **Differential velocities**: stationary habitat vs. spinning rotor
